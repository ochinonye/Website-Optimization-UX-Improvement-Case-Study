01 — Business Overview

Horizon Realty Group

A mid-sized real estate agency specializing in residential property sales, rentals, and property management across the metropolitan area.

What They Do

Horizon Realty Group helps families and professionals buy, sell, and rent residential properties. They offer full-service property management, market analysis, and investment consulting. With 15+ years in the market, they handle everything from first-time homebuyer guidance to portfolio management for investors.

Target Audience

Primary audiences include first-time homebuyers (ages 28–40), relocating professionals, real estate investors, and property owners seeking management services. Most clients discover the business through online search, referrals, and local listings.

Website Purpose

The website serves as the primary digital touchpoint for lead generation, property showcase, and credibility building. It needs to convert visitors into inquiries, establish trust through professionalism, and make property discovery intuitive and engaging.

02 — Website Challenges Identified

What Was Holding Them Back

A comprehensive audit revealed critical issues across layout, messaging, navigation, and trust signals — all contributing to poor user engagement and low inquiry conversion.

🗂️

Cluttered Layout

The homepage featured 8 competing sections above the fold with no visual hierarchy. Property listings, agent bios, testimonials, and a newsletter signup all competed for attention, creating cognitive overload.

🔘

Weak Call-to-Action

The primary CTA — "Contact Us" — was buried in the footer. No clear path guided visitors toward scheduling a consultation or viewing listings. The button styling was inconsistent and visually unremarkable.

📱

Poor Mobile Responsiveness

On mobile devices, text overflowed containers, images stretched awkwardly, and navigation required horizontal scrolling. Over 60% of traffic came from mobile, yet the experience was effectively broken.

↔️

Inconsistent Spacing

Padding and margins varied wildly between sections. Some areas felt cramped while others had excessive whitespace. The lack of a consistent grid system made the site feel unprofessional and hastily assembled.

⏳

Outdated Appearance

The design used gradients and drop shadows popular in 2012, with a serif font pairing that felt dated. The color scheme (bright red and yellow) clashed and undermined the premium positioning of the brand.

🧭

Confusing Navigation

The menu contained 12 items with nested dropdowns three levels deep. Critical pages like "Property Listings" were hidden under "Services," while "About" contained unrelated content mixed with team bios.

🛡️

Weak Trust-Building Elements

No client testimonials were visible on the homepage. There were no trust badges, no recent sales data, no professional certifications displayed, and the "About" page lacked team photos or credentials.

💬

Unclear Messaging

The headline read "Welcome to Horizon Realty" — generic and forgettable. Value propositions were buried in paragraphs of text. Visitors couldn't quickly understand why they should choose this agency over competitors.

06 — UX & Communication Improvements

Design System & Strategic Changes

Detailed breakdown of the UX decisions, design principles, and communication improvements implemented across the redesign.

↔️

Spacing & Layout

Implemented an 8px baseline grid system with consistent vertical rhythm. Section padding standardized to 80px desktop / 48px mobile. Card internal padding set to 32px for breathable content.

Before: Random margins between 12px–60px with no system.
After: 8px grid with predictable spacing scale (8, 16, 24, 32, 48, 64, 80).
🔨

Typography Hierarchy

Established a clear type scale: Display (48–56px) for hero headlines, H2 (32–40px) for section titles, H3 (20–24px) for card headers, and Body (15–16px) for readable paragraphs. Line height optimized to 1.6 for body text.

Before: Single font size (16px) used for everything including headlines.
After: 6-level type scale with Playfair Display for headlines, Inter for body.
👁️

Readability & Contrast

All text meets WCAG AA contrast standards (4.5:1 minimum). Body text color shifted from pure gray (#6b7280) to softer slate (#334155) on white backgrounds. Dark sections use 65% opacity white for comfortable reading.

Before: Light gray text on light backgrounds (2.8:1 contrast).
After: Slate text on white (7.2:1 contrast) — fully accessible.
📊

Visual Hierarchy

Hero section now leads with a compelling value proposition and social proof badge. Services are presented as scannable cards with icons instead of a bulleted list. CTAs use high-contrast buttons with clear action verbs.

Before: 8 competing elements above the fold with equal visual weight.
After: Clear focal point → supporting text → action buttons → secondary content.
🛣️

Customer Journey

Redesigned user flow: Landing → Value Proposition → Services → Social Proof → CTA → Contact. Each step naturally leads to the next with contextual CTAs. No dead ends — every page has a clear next action.

Before: Users landed on generic welcome page with no clear path forward.
After: Intentional funnel from awareness → interest → consideration → action.
💬

Clearer Messaging

Headlines now speak to customer outcomes ("Find Your Perfect Home") rather than company introductions ("Welcome to Horizon Realty"). Value propositions are front-loaded. Every section answers: "What is in it for the visitor?"

Before: "Welcome to Horizon Realty" — company-centric, no value.
After: "Find Your Perfect Home With Confidence" — customer-centric, outcome-focused.
07 — Expected Business Impact

How These Changes Drive Results

Projected business outcomes based on UX best practices, industry benchmarks, and the specific improvements implemented in this redesign.

🤝

Increased Trust

Professional design, social proof badges, and clear credentials signal reliability. Trust is the #1 factor in real estate decisions, and the redesigned site establishes it within 5 seconds of landing.

📈

Improved Engagement

Clear visual hierarchy and intuitive navigation reduce bounce rates and increase time-on-site. Visitors explore more pages, view more listings, and interact with more CTAs.

🌐

Stronger Online Presence

Modern, mobile-responsive design improves SEO rankings and social sharing. A polished website becomes a marketing asset that represents the brand across all digital channels.

💬

Better Customer Communication

Multiple contact touchpoints, clear response expectations, and a simple inquiry form remove friction from the communication process. Clients know exactly how and when to reach out.

🔗

More Inquiries

Strategic CTA placement, compelling value propositions, and reduced friction in the contact process directly increase form submissions and consultation bookings.

⭐

Competitive Differentiation

In a market where most competitors have dated websites, a modern, professional presence immediately positions Horizon Realty as the premium choice for discerning clients.
